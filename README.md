around the globe. But in Cameroon's case, a pool of skilled youth remains struggling to get exposure to

3- Problem Description

The emergence of digital freelancing has greatly influenced how people and companies interact with each other in terms of knowledge acquisition and service delivery across digital borders. In well-structured economies around the globe, digital freelancers are provided with sophisticated online enabling environments that include secure project delivery sites, collaborative online platforms, safe online payment channels, and extensive online marketplaces that enable them to connect with clients from across the world. Unfortunately, these conditions are not experienced by digital freelancers in Cameroon.

Although there has been extensive development in terms of online connectivity in Cameroon, with increased access to the internet as well as increased use of smartphones, there exists inadequate infrastructure in terms of a supportive environment for online collaboration between freelancers. There are numerous challenges that freelancers face that hinder online collaboration. These are both technological and system-related dimensions that pose a complex issue that needs to be solved using an innovative approach.

3.1 Fragmented Collaboration

The first major issue to address is that there are no collaborative platforms. In Cameroon, freelancers communicate using common platforms like WhatsApp Messenger or Facebook Messenger. Although these enable basic communication, there are not features to manage complex tasks like project or document collaboration. Due to these limitations, project coordination between freelancers becomes chaotic. When freelancers are involved in a project, project coordination remains inefficient.

For example, a freelance graphics designer could get a contract offer from a local businessman over WhatsApp. They could later show them screenshots of the project's progress using the same WhatsApp. In case the businessman doesn't honor the contract terms concerning payment, there won't be a system in place to resolve possible disputes. Such a common practice amongst thousands of freelancers makes engaging in freelance jobs less credible.

3.2 Limited Access to Opportunities

The other significant issue is a lack of access to clients or projects. Unlike other global markets like Upwork or Fiverr, which provide access to global project listings or clients, in Cameroon there isn't a central marketplace where freelancers are directly linked to clients. In fact, access to projects for most skilled youth happens randomly either through referrals or announcements on online forums or social networks. Again, there's inequality in access to projects where only a few are well-connected to get jobs while others are under- or unemployed.

In addition to these issues, freelancers in Cameroon face challenges when trying to access international freelance markets. These challenges include restrictions on payments made to freelancers, inadequate methods for establishing verified identities for users, and the difficulty of competing with international freelancers who possess improved online reputation and infrastructure. Indeed, the inability for freelancers to access international freelance markets not only limits earning capacities but hinders collaboration.

3.3. Payment and Trust Issues

One of the major problems that need to be addressed is related to the lack of secure payment options. In Cameroon, freelancers use various methods for payment. They involve direct bank payments or mobile money. These methods are not tied to project monitoring. As a result, there are dangers associated with possible fraud. In fact, there are times when clients pay after doing part of the project. Sometimes freelancers cannot commit to doing the project if there is no initial payment.

In addition to these challenges, other issues are raised in terms of integrating payments due to the remoteness or non-availability of international digital payment gateways like PayPal, Stripe, or Payoneer. These are either not available or not so reliable in Cameroon. It makes it difficult for freelancers to get paid or to partner with international clients. In turn, international collaboration opportunities are missed, and freelancers are thus limited to carrying out small projects.

3.4 Technological Limitations and Scalability Challenges

Even amongst those who are trying to build online freelancing platforms locally, there are technological issues related to scalability. Many startups in local jurisdictions use a monolithic design that runs on shared servers. Scaling these designs can result in ineffectual platforms compared to those running global freelance platforms. Also, there isn't sufficient knowledge amongst those in innovation to design platforms which incorporate distributed systems like microservices. Such issues constitute one major challenge to building freelance platforms.

The issue also encompasses data integrity and security. With most platforms adopting a central database system, there is a risk that a single point of failure—either hardware failure or network downtime—may result in system failure. In a state where internet uptime or electricity availability are not guaranteed, these systems are highly unreliable. In fact, there lies a great need for a system that distributes data across nodes or regions to achieve high availability.

3.5 Lack of Collaborative Culture and Verification Mechanisms

Interactions between freelancers are also made difficult due to a lack of structures for validation or verification. In conventional methods used in freelance jobs, freelancers undertake tasks alone. Specialized projects like developing apps, online marketing campaigns, or creating videos involve diverse groups working simultaneously. These groups cannot function because there are no structures to validate tasks or assign them.

Moreover, there are no validation mechanisms to check who has contributed what to a team assignment. Sometimes, there are issues arising from sharing credits and compensations. The fact that there are no reputation mechanisms suggests that freelancers cannot establish reputation profiles to enable new members to prove themselves. Apart from these technology-related challenges that make freelancing tough in Cameroon, there are other socioeconomic factors. The cost of using the internet in Cameroon is relatively high compared to its income. Additionally, fixed broadband access is not yet universal. In fact, some freelancers resort to mobile data networks that are not tailored to real-time collaboration or large data transmission. Finally, because there are no reliable local options for hosting startups, these startups become dependent on cloud services provided by international companies. These are some structural issues which result in digital exclusion, specifically with freelancer communities in rural areas. Although youth in cities are partly exposed to digital resources, those who belong to less-connected zones are largely left out of the digital economy. A distributed system like TalentSync could offer a solution to address this problem by offering services across a variety of nodes with access models that suit different connection speeds.

5- Proposed Solution (Distributed Systems Integration)

5.1. Overview of Proposed Solution

The TalentSync platform offers a model for a distributed freelance collaboration platform in the cloud to allow Cameroonian youth to connect with project teams to serve clients. The TalentSync platform uses principles like scalability, fault tolerance, concurrency, or decentralized coordination found in distributed systems to enable efficient service delivery, including in areas like Cameroon where infrastructure development is precisely little.

In other words, TalentSync will not use a common central server. Rather, TalentSync will use a microservices design across cloud platforms. In this design, separate self-owned services (like user service, project service, chat service, payment service), using APIs, communicate with one another. Also, these self-owned services are free to scale according to requirements.

The adoption of cloud computing makes sure that there is flexibility in resource usage, cost savings, and access for both developer and user sides. With the integration of distributed databases, message queues, and microservices, TalentSync will prove that efficient design based on distributed technology principles can address freelance collaboration issues in Africa.

5.2 Solution Design

5.4 Principles of Distributed System Applications

Now we'll see how distributed computing technology-specifically its concepts-addresses identified issues in Cameroon's freelance industry.

5.4.1 Scalability

Services can be independently scaled using microservices. The chat service can scale up to handle peak collaboration periods without interfering with user authentication or payment. Cloud Scaling makes sure that everything functions well in terms of user traffic.

5.4.2 Fault Tolerance and High Availability

Distributed Replication makes sure that no matter which node fails, functionality isn't compromised. PostgreSQL databases can, for example, use master-replica configurations. It becomes possible to replace a failed instance with a new container in Kubernetes automatically.

5.4.3 Concurrency

Asynchronous APIs enable multiple users to execute tasks at the same time (e.g., chat functionality, file uploads, payments). This is crucial in team settings where numerous users get to interact simultaneously.

5.4.4 Consistency and Coordination

Two phase commit (2PC) or wait-for-eventual-consistency models are applicable to services. In fact, a payment update generates an event which synchronizes later with project status after validation.

5.4.5 Transparency and Decentralization

In fact, task completion validation can separately exist without involving a central system with simulator blockchain/distributed ledger technology. This makes it transparent. It limits the scope for manipulation in payments to freelancers.

5.4.6 Security and Encryption

Every microservices system implements its authentication and authorization mechanism using JWT. Data that requires confidentiality (such as transaction data) will utilize AES-256 encryption and will be stored in secure nodes. Cloud-based Identity Access Management (IAM) provides security at a service level.
